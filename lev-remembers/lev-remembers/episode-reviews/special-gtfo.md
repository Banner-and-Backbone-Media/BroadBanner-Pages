# Lev Remembers | Special - GTFO

## Defiance and Project Saltbox launch GTFO — gtfoice.org — an early-warning network that lets neighborhoods kill ICE detention centers before the beds get built.

### Lev Remembers In Review

Lev opens on the day's breaking news — James Comey arraigned and released on his own recognizance — then hands the floor to Bre Gurosko, an Army veteran and Project Saltbox co-founder out of Baltimore, and Xander Schultz of Defiance.org. The pitch is GTFO, accessible at gtfoice.org: a rapid-response network that alerts subscribers by zip code the moment an ICE detention center is being planned in their area, and ships them a playbook to stop it. Bre's reporting is the spine of the operation. She traced the build-out by scraping USA Spending and SAM.gov, mapped purchases in Chicago and L.A. into a pattern, and cross-referenced leaked DHS documents from the Washington Post that revealed the warehouse strategy. Xander frames the editorial stakes plainly: the fight against mass incarceration is won by limiting beds. If DHS can't site the buildings, the rest of the policing and deportation machine has nowhere to put anyone — Steve Schmidt's Auschwitz comparison from last week's launch is the historical analogue Xander leans on.

The operational thesis is that boring bureaucratic interventions work. Bre walks through Williamsport, where the state attorney general blocked a warehouse sale because DHS skipped environmental review and missed a 10-day public-comment window — Project Saltbox responded by spinning up a mass-email tool to flood the comment record. In Social Circle, Georgia, the city manager simply turned off the water on a $100 million DHS-funded warehouse. Xander argues DHS made a political miscalculation: betting that conservative towns would welcome detention centers, then learning that NIMBY beats partisanship every time. Bre flags the warning signs of a coming surge — Cellebrite digital-forensics purchases at multiple field offices, a 300,000-MRE request to Baltimore (which is a 12-hour holding office on paper, recently extended to 72), and ICE flight cadence at BWI rising to twice a week from zero. Lev closes by anchoring the episode in his own "Enough Is Enough" stance: this is not a fundraising drive, it's a movement-building drive. Sign up at gtfoice.org, become a Defiance member for a dollar a month, and as Bre puts it, stop being afraid — the administration's plan only works if the public stays uninformed.

### Organizations and Programs

- gtfoice.org / GTFO (Get The Facilities Out) — early-warning detection network for planned ICE detention centers; pairs zip-code alerts with a stage-by-stage intervention playbook.
- Project Saltbox — Bre's research operation that built the warehouse tracker by working USA Spending and SAM.gov filings.
- Defiance.org — Xander's organization; weekly Mission Brief on Wednesdays funds and amplifies pro-democracy efforts. Membership is a buck a month.
- Save America — partner organization synthesized into the GTFO playbook alongside Project Saltbox.
- DHS — the federal buyer driving the warehouse build-out; the entity GTFO is engineered to delay and bleed in court.
- Worker Circle — Ariane Betancourt's group running Saturday vigils tied to Alligator Alcatraz.

### Terms and Concepts

- Stages of intervention — pre-purchase (community tip / public-pressure on sellers), post-purchase pre-operational (lawsuits, environmental review, water/energy/land rights), and post-operational (court accompaniment, ISAP check-ins, flight watching).
- NIMBY-beats-partisanship — Xander's read on why conservative towns are still rejecting detention centers in their backyards.
- Williamsport warehouse — case study where bypassed environmental review handed the AG the legal opening to sue.
- Alligator Alcatraz — Florida tent facility in the Everglades; Lev's local benchmark for "what happens when you don't stop them in time."
- Cellebrite — digital-forensics software whose multi-site procurement Bre cites as evidence ICE is preparing to ramp back up.
- A dollar a day keeps Trump away — Lev's tagline for sustained, distributed grassroots support.
